Project 1: Manufacturing Technology Curriculum Package

Situation

In response to the increasing demand for skilled professionals in the manufacturing industry, there was a need to create comprehensive virtual training courses that catered to various skill levels, from beginners to advanced. The target audience included secondary and post-secondary students as well as industry professionals. Additionally, collaboration with industry experts was essential to ensure the courses met industry standards and incorporated the latest technological advancements.

Task

I was tasked with designing, developing, implementing, and maintaining a comprehensive curriculum package of virtual courses for manufacturing technology. The objective was to create over 30 courses that catered to various skill levels, from beginner to advanced. Additionally, I collaborated with Subject Matter Experts (SMEs) from leading companies in the manufacturing industry to design and implement over 20 specialized courses. These courses focused on multiple machine tools, including Haas, Fanuc, and HURCO, as well as Computer-Aided Design (CAD) software like SolidWorks, and Computer-Aided Manufacturing (CAM) software such as Autodesk Fusion 360 and Mastercam. I also maintained these courses over time, ensuring they remained up-to-date with technological advancements.

Action

I approached the design of each course by leveraging a combination of instructional design models, including ADDIE, SAM, and KEMP. Each of these models contributed to different phases of course development.

To ensure each course was outcome-driven and practical, I utilized Action Mapping. This approach helped me design courses with clear, measurable goals that directly addressed the skills and knowledge learners needed. The goals for each course were designed to ensure that learners could apply essential skills and knowledge to real-world scenarios, demonstrating their proficiency through measurable performance benchmarks and practical assessments. By starting the design process with these measurable goals, I ensured that every course was focused on producing specific, actionable outcomes for learners.

To develop the virtual training courses, I utilized a range of tools, including Adobe Captivate, Adobe Flash Professional, Snagit, Camtasia, and Immersive Engineering's proprietary authoring system. The courses were deployed through Immersive Engineering's proprietary LMS, and iSpring was used for SCORM-compliant publishing. I incorporated engaging elements such as gamification and video, integrating interactive activities designed to captivate and engage learners. Additionally, I served as an application engineer and SME in developing a desktop application, a CNC machine emulator. I integrated the emulator into the virtual CNC courses, which allowed students to practice their skills and complete assignments in a virtual setting when real-world machines were not practical.

Maintaining these courses over time was an ongoing responsibility. I implemented a continuous improvement process that included frequent updates based on software advancements, industry trends, assessment reports, and user analytics. Quality checklists were used throughout this process to ensure that each course met high standards of accuracy, effectiveness, and user engagement. Collaboration with SMEs from leading manufacturing companies was key to integrating up-to-date industry knowledge and ensuring the technical accuracy of each course. This approach ensured that the entire curriculum remained relevant and effective, adapting to the ever-changing technological landscape.

Ensuring accessibility was a key priority in the course development process. To achieve this, I employed a detailed quality checklist to verify compliance with WCAG 2.0 standards. This checklist guided the evaluation and adjustment of course materials to ensure they were accessible to all learners, including those with disabilities. By systematically applying these accessibility standards, I was able to create an inclusive learning environment that met the needs of a diverse audience.

Result

The result was a robust curriculum of over 50 virtual courses, 30 of which I independently designed and developed, with the remaining 20 created in collaboration with industry experts. The courses effectively addressed the skills gap in the manufacturing industry, with assessments and feedback indicating that learners were able to apply their new skills in real-world scenarios. Collaboration with SMEs ensured the inclusion of up-to-date industry knowledge and technical accuracy, which was well-received by stakeholders. The continuous improvement process, incorporating frequent updates and adherence to quality checklists, maintained the curriculum’s relevance and effectiveness over time. The success of the courses was demonstrated not only by retaining existing customers but also by achieving approximately 1,100% growth in the customer base over a 15-year period, reflecting the increased reach and impact of the curriculum within the industry.

Project 2: Detroit Youth Summer Camp: Interactive Virtual Training Program

Situation

Tailor virtual training to specific audiences with strict deadlines.

Task

I was faced with the challenge of implementing a hybrid course for a Detroit Youth Summer Program (12–14-year-olds). I had one week to design, develop, and implement a tailored hybrid manufacturing technology course for a younger audience than usual. I had no prior knowledge of the learners' proficiencies.

Action

I followed the ADDIE model for instructional design and incorporated elements from other design methods as needed.

  • Analyze: I met with program directors to obtain the learners' profiles, analyzing their strengths, weaknesses, proficiency, and interests. This allowed me to identify common interests and core educational strengths among the learners.
  • Design: Based on the analysis, I designed content with fun, interactive activities tailored to the learners' proficiencies and interests. Each virtual learning module concluded with a project that matched their skill levels and aligned with their interests.
  • Develop: I developed a virtual training program using Adobe Captivate, Adobe Flash Professional, and Camtasia. The courses incorporated engaging gamification and video, integrating fun, interactive activities that engaged learners by aligning with their proficiencies and interests. I ensured the courses met WCAG 2.0 compliance for accessibility and conducted thorough testing to ensure a seamless user experience.
  • Implement: I implemented the program through the company’s LMS. The learners were assigned eLearning modules each week prior to attending in-person classes.
  • In-process Evaluation: Prior to each in-person class, I used LMS assessment reports to evaluate student performance. Based on metrics such as assessment scores, engagement time, and drop-off rates, I adjusted the following week's virtual learning assignments.

Result

The class was a resounding success, demonstrated by a significant percentage increase between pre- and post-assessment scores, reflecting the learners' substantial knowledge gain. Unexpectedly, many participants also acquired the skills to operate precision CNC machine tools, a testament to the course's practical effectiveness. Positive feedback from learner surveys further reinforced the success, with participants praising the course’s engaging content. Additionally, the project included community outreach, which engaged local youth and strengthened the connection between the program and the community, making the training more impactful.

Project 3: Reduce Customer Support Inquiries

Situation

At Immersive Engineering Inc., the growing customer base, combined with the addition of new LMS administrative tools such as student evaluation reports, certification settings, and learning resources, led to a higher volume of recurring inquiries regarding LMS administration tools. This increase in inquiries was straining our customer support team and resulting in customer frustration.

Task

My task was to develop and globally deploy a virtual course to address and resolve the recurring inquiries related to LMS administration tools. The goal was to create an accessible and comprehensive resource that would help reduce the volume of support inquiries, alleviate customer frustration, and ensure consistent training for all LMS administrators.

Action

I utilized the ADDIE model of instructional design to create and globally deploy the virtual course.

  • Analyze: I analyzed customer support data and feedback to identify key areas needing attention.
  • Design: I designed a video-based virtual course to demonstrate LMS administration processes, including the LMS interface, student registration, course assignments, peer administration registration, student evaluation using reports, virtual communication tools, course resources, course navigation, and desktop application installation, and use.
  • Develop: I developed the video-based virtual training course using Camtasia and Immersive Engineering Inc.’s proprietary authoring system. I ensured the course was WCAG 2.0 compliant for accessibility and conducted thorough testing to verify functionality and user experience.
  • Implement: I deployed the course globally to all LMS administrators using an automated LMS function. The course was made easily accessible upon login by featuring it prominently on their home page. The automated LMS function deployed courses based on user privileges, ensuring that administrators received consistent and widespread training. Additionally, administrators were automatically notified by email of the updates, ensuring they were promptly informed about the new course availability.
  • Evaluate: I used internal analytical tools to determine course engagement metrics, including how many administrators accessed the course and which lessons were completed. By examining these metrics, I identified areas of the LMS that needed to be more intuitive. This data-driven approach allowed me to make targeted improvements to enhance the user experience and address areas where administrators struggled.

Result

The evaluation process revealed that most administrators needed more awareness of the desktop application, course resources, certification settings, and various student evaluation reports. By targeting these specific areas for improvement, support inquiries dramatically decreased, customer satisfaction increased, and overall product usage saw a significant rise. The targeted enhancements helped ensure administrators were better informed and more proficient in using the LMS, leading to a more efficient experience.
